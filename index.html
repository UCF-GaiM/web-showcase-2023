Mashed

Team

  • Naomy Noguez
  • Alan Nguyen
  • Laura Melo
  • Shelby Constant

Description

Our team invested countless hours creating Mashed, a social recipe-sharing app that embodies our love for food and community. Over the course of 9 months and 8 milestones, we worked hard to develop a user-friendly interface with easy-to-use social interactions that encourage content sharing and connection. We aimed to allow people to share/edit recipes, explore new cuisines, and challenge themselves and others to cooking challenges. Shelby and Laura played a pivotal role in shaping the app's visual aesthetic and content strategy, while Naomy and Alan tackled Front and Back-end Development. We worked tirelessly to ensure that the app's core functionality was implemented seamlessly, including sign-ups, logins, recipe sharing, and user identification, and accurately portrayed the designs created by Shelby and Laura. To connect the app to the database, we used React Native and PHP to send JSON data between the two and connect to a MySQL database to store and display user data. The result was a full-fledged recipe application that parsed through various recipe and food data to create a memorable experience. Together, we are proud to have created a platform that brings people together through food and encourages exploration, creativity, and community building.

Vacation Base

Team

  • Naomy Noguez
  • Alan Nguyen
  • Laura Melo
  • Shelby Constant

Description

Over the course of two semesters, our group collaborated on a project to create a travel itinerary website called Vacation Base. The first semester was focused on the design phase while the second semester was dedicated to building the website. Our goal was to create a platform that would provide travelers with a one-stop shop for planning their trips.

Our user-friendly homepage houses categorized activities inside dynamic carousels such as Theme Park and Restaurants. One of the core features of Vacation Base is the Idea Base, where users can browse through a wide range of activities and attractions. This includes pre-planned itineraries, travel tips, and blogs to help users plan their trips. To make the planning process even easier, we developed an itinerary builder with a drag-and-drop feature that allows users to customize their itinerary based on their preferences. We also added a search page with a variety of filters, such as minimum age, budget, family-friendly and opening hours. We also included a profile page where users can create and edit their profile and view their previously saved itineraries. We used HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for the front end, and PHP and SQL to handle server-side tasks.

Throughout the project, we worked collaboratively by dividing tasks based on our strengths and interests and supported each other throughout the process. By the end of the project, we were proud to have created a functional and user-friendly website that we believe will help travelers plan memorable vacations.

Vallartas Mexican Restaurant

Team

  • Shlok Jani - Group Leader and Documenter
  • Fransheska Nunez - Front-end Programmer
  • Mayte Ortega-Olmos - Copywriter and Content Creator
  • Christopher Gogolinksi - Back-end Programmer
  • Nathan Parrett - Graphic Designer and Photo Editor

Description

Vallarta' Mexican Restaurant Website is a redesign of an existing webpage that contains essential information for a chain of Mexican restaurants. The original site lacked organization, basic services, and accessibility practices. When first tackling this redesign, we made note of what makes other similar sites successful and convenient to use. We then added functioning essential services such as a reservation system, a personal account setup function, location maps, and online ordering. The entire website was rebranded with a new color scheme, a new logo, and corresponding styling. Our redesign is a responsive website that works best on desktop full screen, but it also works well in mobile view. We operated a user-centered approach that kept functionality as its top priority.

Joybird Books

Team

  • Trisha Cooper - Full-Stack Developer: Specialized in integrating front-end and back-end components.
  • Cassandra Ortega - Technical Writer: Specialized in creating comprehensive documentation, user guides, and content for the platform.
  • Alexis Yarbrough - Quality Assurance Engineer: Ensured the platform meets the required quality standards, performed testing, and addressed any quality issues.
  • Mallory Bronson - Frontend Developer: Focused on developing user interface screens and components.
  • Al Waleed Al Khodhuri - UI/UI Designer: Designed the user interface and user experience, optimized the platform's aesthetics, usability, and styled responsiveness.

Description

Our product goal is bringing a local bookstore to the digital market. The goal with Joybird Books is creating a comprehensive online platform dedicated to providing their customers with the best user experience. Offering Joybird Books customers an efficient access to products, store events and spotlighting local authors. Our primary objective with Joybirds Books website is creating an attractive and responsive ecommerce web application compatible with various web browsers and modern technology, fostering a community of bibliophiles to explore new titles and support local emerging writers. To achieve our goal, we ensured that our website features up-to-date information, implemented advanced ecommerce features such as sorting, filtrations, and a seamless checkout process. In addition to that we successfully implemented a user-friendly custom admin panel enabling the merchant to control important aspects of the website such as managing users, orders, and reviews without the need of a dedicated developer. Overall, we successfully achieved our desired product goals by aligning the business and the customers' needs. Creating a unique and user-friendly ecommerce website for Joybirds Books. This accomplishment was made possible through the efficient collaboration of our diverse team members, whose combined skills and expertise contributed to the seamless execution of the project.

RideRaters

Team

  • Sarah Onken -- main researcher, writer, and Project Manager.
  • Nico Perez -- lead coder and developer
  • Michael Makhmudov -- assistant coder and backend developer
  • Kayla Lares -- main designer and assistant researcher
  • Jewel Dias -- graphic designer and assistant researcher

Description

RideRaters is a theme park attraction rating app that rates attractions from various theme parks located in Florida. The focus is on accessibility, personalization, list creation, and ease of access to information. Users should feel comfortable and confident when deciding on what theme park attractions would best suit their visit by offering mobile 'on the go' quick searching while on vacation and within the theme parks themselves. The project is both an innovation of a theme park rollercoaster review and blogging applications, as this is an untapped market that has not seen many applications. Incorporating HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and MySQL, enables our application to employ consistent style and responsiveness for a successful user experience.
